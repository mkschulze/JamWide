# Cross-AI Plan Review — Phase 15.1 (RT-Safety Hardening)

## Codex Review

### Summary

This is a strong, audit-driven plan set with good traceability, sensible early sequencing, and much better verification discipline than most RT-hardening plans. But several of the hardest claims are not actually closed by the mechanics as written. The two biggest problems are: `15.1-09` does **not** truly remove the file-backed `runDecode`/`fread` path from the audio thread after initial arming, and `15.1-06`/`15.1-07` rely on transitional raw-pointer bridges (`lc_ptr`, `user_ptr`) whose lifetime contract is under-specified and can reintroduce UAF/race risk while claiming the audio path is "mirror-only." So the plan is directionally right, but not yet airtight enough to guarantee the stated RT-safety bar.

### Strengths

- The phase is grounded in a real call-graph audit, not intuition.
  - That materially lowers the chance of missing a hot-path violation.
- The sequencing is mostly sensible.
  - `15.1-02` atomic warm-up, `15.1-03` pure deletion, `15.1-04` infra, then integration steps is the right shape.
- Verification is much stronger than average.
  - Auditor reruns, TSan, explicit grep-based goal-backward checks, and manual populated-server UAT are all appropriate.
- The plans correctly treat "call-site integration" as in-scope.
  - That closes the common loophole where codec/blocking work is declared "someone else's problem."
- Deferred delete is the right primitive for `DecodeState`.
  - Audio-thread deallocation is one of the most concrete and high-value fixes here.
- The plans repeatedly call out where assumptions are being made.
  - That makes adversarial review possible, which is a good sign.

### Concerns

- **[HIGH] `15.1-09` does not actually close H-04 as written.**
  - `15.1-09` moves `start_decode` and the initial fill loop off-thread, but `mixInChannel` still calls `chan->runDecode(256)` on a FILE-backed `DecodeState`. Once the initial buffered samples are consumed, that path still reaches `fread(decode_fp)` on the audio thread. The plan's claim that H-04 becomes "structurally unreachable" is not supported by the described mechanics.
- **[HIGH] `15.1-06` and `15.1-07` use raw-pointer escape hatches that undermine the mirror model.**
  - `15.1-06` introduces `lc_ptr`; `15.1-07` introduces `user_ptr`. That means the audio thread is still reaching into run-thread-owned objects after claiming it is now using an audio-thread-owned mirror. Without a hard lifetime barrier, this is a race/UAF hazard, especially during delete/remove churn.
- **[HIGH] The lifetime contract for peer/local object removal is incomplete.**
  - `15.1-07` threat `T-15.1-07-07` correctly identifies that peer removal needs deferred free after the audio thread has consumed `PeerRemovedUpdate`, but the plan does not actually add a concrete task implementing that for `RemoteUser`/`Local_Channel`. The risk is identified, not closed.
- **[HIGH] `15.1-07` is too large and too transitional to be safely "one sub-plan."**
  - CR-01, CR-09, CR-10, and CR-12 are each major rewrites. Bundling mirror migration, buffer transport replacement, and decode-buffer backend replacement into one landing makes regressions hard to isolate and violates the "one violation, one commit-equivalent" spirit.
- **[MEDIUM] The TSan "single-target" story still does not automate the actual audio callback path.**
  - `--tsan` building the standalone is useful, but `ctest` does not itself drive `processBlock` against a real session. So ROADMAP S1 ("TSan build ... passes a NINJAM session simulation with peer churn") is not actually satisfied by the automated pieces as written; the real callback coverage remains manual UAT.
- **[MEDIUM] `BlockRecord` Design A may trade one RT problem for excessive audio-thread memcpy cost.**
  - `15.1-07` hardcodes `BlockRecord` as an inline ~16KB payload and copies it through the ring. That is lock-free, but not obviously cheap under many local channels / high block rate. The plan proves safety better than performance.
- **[MEDIUM] `MAX_BLOCK_SAMPLES = 2048` is assumed too early and too globally.**
  - `15.1-04` bakes this into payload infrastructure before `15.1-08` adds the host-block-size assertion. If a host legitimately drives larger buffers, `15.1-07` will silently truncate or drop unless every callsite checks bounds rigorously.
- **[MEDIUM] Queue overflow policy is weak in several places.**
  - `15.1-05` explicitly accepts leaking `DecodeState*` on deferred-delete overflow.
  - `15.1-07` silently drops `BlockRecord`s.
  - `15.1-09` silently drops arm requests except for a counter.
  - For RT that may be acceptable locally, but the phase-close verification should fail if any of these counters are nonzero.
- **[MEDIUM] Cross-plan payload/API churn is already visible.**
  - `15.1-04` defines payloads one way, then `15.1-06` wants to extend `LocalChannelAddedUpdate` with `lc_ptr`, and `15.1-07` likely needs richer peer-add payload than the infra plan defines. That suggests the "stable Wave 0 infra" is not actually stable.
- **[LOW] Some tests validate weaker properties than the plan text implies.**
  - Example: `15.1-02` atomic tests do not really prove correctness of the real publication protocol under missed-update semantics; they mostly prove "no obvious TSan race."

### Per-Plan Notes

- **15.1-02** — Mostly sound. Should explicitly state whether `m_beatinfo_updated` is "edge-triggered best effort" or "last-value latch" — the reader clearing the flag can lose intermediate updates.
- **15.1-03** — Good deletion plan. Consider removing or compile-guarding dead `writeUserChanLog` entirely after callers are gone, rather than leaving inert code behind.
- **15.1-04** — Good infra phase, but it hardens payload shapes too early. Later plans already want to mutate those shapes, which defeats the point of a clean infra landing.
- **15.1-05** — Deferred delete is correct, but "overflow => leak" needs a hard verification rule. If `m_deferred_delete_q` ever overflows in testing, that should fail the phase, not just incrementally leak.
- **15.1-06** — The shakiest "independent" sub-plan. The `lc_ptr` bridge means the audio thread still dereferences canonical `Local_Channel` objects. That weakens the claim that CR-02 is really solved architecturally here.
- **15.1-07** — Highest implementation risk after `15.1-09`. The peer mirror, block transport replacement, and decode-buffer rewrite each deserve their own blast-radius containment. As written, debugging failures here will be painful.
- **15.1-08** — The dependency on `15.1-09` is correct. Test details that inspect decoder internals may be awkward if those fields aren't publicly accessible; the plan should acknowledge that and allow a lighter sanity test.
- **15.1-09** — The biggest logic gap. Moving `start_decode` off-thread does not, by itself, move subsequent file-backed decode reads off-thread. The plan overclaims closure of H-04.
- **15.1-10** — Good closure structure, but ROADMAP S1 still lacks a real automated "peer churn simulation" tied to the callback path. Right now that evidence is split between ctest and manual UAT.

### Suggestions

- **Split `15.1-07`.** At minimum: `07a` remote-user mirror/lifetime, `07b` BufferQueue replacement, `07c` DecodeMediaBuffer backend. The current plan is too wide.
- **Fix `15.1-09` before implementation.** If H-04 truly must close, you need one of:
  - full sessionmode decode/refill off audio thread, or
  - a file-reader thread/run-thread refill queue feeding the audio thread, or
  - predecoded/session-buffered data such that audio-thread `runDecode` never touches `FILE*`.
  - The current "initial arming only" approach is insufficient.
- **Eliminate raw-pointer bridges as a success-state.** `lc_ptr`/`user_ptr` can be temporary scaffolding during development, but they should not be the shipped design for a phase claiming architectural RT-safety.
- **Add explicit lifetime tasks for `RemoteUser` and `Local_Channel`.** Not just `DecodeState`. Removal must be publish-remove-update first, then deferred free after audio-thread visibility is guaranteed.
- **Make queue-drop counters part of acceptance.**
  - `deferred_delete_overflows == 0`
  - `arm_request_drops == 0`
  - `block_queue_drops == 0`
  - If not zero under UAT/TSan, the phase is not actually "clean."
- **Revisit `BlockRecord` transport design.** A fixed pool + handle ring is more complex, but safer on CPU than copying ~16KB records through SPSC for every push.
- **Tighten the verification wording.** Separate:
  - "TSan unit/integration test suite passes"
  - "manual standalone callback-path UAT under TSan passes"
  - "automated peer-churn session simulation exists"
  - Right now those are blended.

### Risk Assessment

**Overall risk: HIGH**

The plan set is thoughtful, but two of the central RT-safety claims are still shaky: `15.1-09` does not actually remove ongoing FILE-backed decode I/O from the audio thread, and `15.1-06`/`15.1-07` depend on transitional raw-pointer access patterns whose lifetime safety is not fully designed. Those are not cosmetic issues; they go directly to whether the roadmap success criteria are genuinely achievable.

**Single highest-risk plan:** `15.1-09-codec-call-site-integration-PLAN.md`

Because as written it appears to "close" CR-08/H-04 on paper while still leaving a plausible audio-thread `fread` path alive in steady-state sessionmode playback. That is the most dangerous kind of failure here: a false-green architectural claim.

## Consensus Summary

> **Note:** Only one external reviewer (Codex) was available — Claude was skipped for independence (host runtime), and Gemini/CodeRabbit/OpenCode/Qwen/Cursor are not installed locally. There is no cross-AI consensus to synthesize. The themes below are extracted from the single Codex review and should be weighed on merits, not corroborated agreement.

### Themes Codex Returned To Repeatedly

- **Architectural false-greens.** Plans `15.1-09`, `15.1-06`, and `15.1-07` each declare a CR/H finding "closed" but leave a plausible path where the underlying violation can still occur (file `fread` on audio thread; raw-pointer dereferences into run-thread-owned objects). Severity: HIGH.
- **Lifetime contracts only half-specified.** `DecodeState` has deferred-delete; `RemoteUser` and `Local_Channel` do not, even though removal-while-audio-thread-uses-mirror-pointer is the same hazard. Severity: HIGH.
- **Wave 0 infra (15.1-04) isn't actually stable.** Subsequent plans already want to mutate the payload shapes the infra plan locks in. Severity: MEDIUM.
- **TSan automation gap.** ROADMAP S1 ("TSan ... peer churn simulation") is not actually delivered by the planned ctest scope; it slips to manual UAT. Severity: MEDIUM.
- **Drop-counter discipline.** Three queues (deferred-delete, arm-request, block-queue) tolerate silent drops. Phase verification doesn't currently fail on nonzero counters. Severity: MEDIUM.

### Single Highest-Priority Action

Rework `15.1-09` before executing it. The current "arm `start_decode` off-thread" mechanism does not eliminate steady-state file `fread` from `runDecode` on the audio thread, so claiming H-04 closed is incorrect.

### Secondary Action

Split `15.1-07` into `07a` (remote-user mirror + lifetime), `07b` (BufferQueue replacement), `07c` (DecodeMediaBuffer backend) so the largest single-plan blast radius in the phase is reduced before execution.
