**1. Instatalk Channel Visible to Other Users**

**Test:** Connect two JamWide clients to the same NINJAM server. On client B, open the mixer. Verify a channel named "Instatalk" appears in Client A's remote user strip.
**Expected:** "Instatalk" channel is visible at channel index 4 with flags=0x02 (instamode encoding)

**2. Measured vs Calculated Footer Transition**

**Test:** With two JamWide clients in the same NINJAM room, open the companion page on one client. Watch the footer delay display while both clients are connected.
**Expected:** Footer initially shows "X.Xs (calculated)". After Instatalk probe measurement completes, footer transitions to "X.Xs (measured)".
**Why human:** Requires two live JamWide clients. The measurement state machine (IDLE->INSTA_CAPTURED->MEASURED->CONSUMED) and its integration with the run thread are verified in code, but the end-to-end timing and race conditions require runtime confirmation.

**3. Syncing Overlay Browser Behavior**

**Test:** Trigger a measured delay (two-client scenario above). Observe the companion page video area immediately after measured delay arrives.
**Expected:** A black overlay reading "Syncing video..." appears over the video area, then fades out after the delay period expires.
**Why human:** jsdom tests confirm overlay creation/removal in DOM, but the CSS transition (opacity fade), z-index stacking over VDO.Ninja iframe, and setTimeout timing require a real browser environment.

**4. PTT Silence Routing**

**Test:** With JamWide connected to NINJAM, do NOT hold the PTT key. Monitor the Instatalk channel signal on the NINJAM server side.
**Expected:** Instatalk channel transmits silence (zeroed buffer) when PTT is inactive, so it doesn't disrupt the session with audio bleed.
**Why human:** The lambda callback zeroing the buffer is verified in code (NinjamRunThread.cpp line 375), but whether the zeroed buffer correctly propagates through the NINJAM audio pipeline without any bleed requires live audio hardware and a server-side monitoring tool.
