## Deviations from Plan

### Deviation 1: `[Rule 1 - Bug] Plan instruction to remove guidtostr was overbroad`

- **Found during:** Task 1 — pre-edit grep audit of `guidtostr` callers.
- **Issue:** Plan step 4 said "guidtostr was reached only via writeUserChanLog ... after step 3, it has zero callers. Apply the same treatment ... OR delete outright (preferred)." This is incorrect — `grep -n 'guidtostr\b'` shows callers at lines 523 (`guidtostr_tmp`), 566 (`makeFilenameFromGuid`), 1559 (sessionlog), 1691, 1831 (localsessionlog). All are non-audio-thread paths. Removing `guidtostr` would have broken the run-thread session-log writers.
- **Root cause:** The audit's L-02 finding correctly said "Once `writeUserChanLog` is removed (per H-02 fix) this becomes irrelevant" — meaning irrelevant *on the audio thread*. The plan misread that as "remove `guidtostr` entirely."
- **Fix:** Retain `guidtostr` unchanged. The audit's intent (no `guidtostr` on the audio thread) is satisfied because it's only ever called from non-audio paths now.
- **Files modified:** none (decision NOT to modify a file).
- **Commit:** N/A (decision not action).

### Deviation 2: `[Rule 2 - Critical functionality] Surrounding if-block deleted with the JAMWIDE_DEV_BUILD body`

- **Found during:** Task 1 — analyzing the H-01 fopen block in context.
- **Issue:** The plan instructed deleting only the inner `#ifdef JAMWIDE_DEV_BUILD ... #endif` block (lines 2152-2160). But the surrounding `if (!m_debug_logged_remote && m_remoteusers.GetSize() > 0)` (lines 2145-2161) existed solely to one-shot that dev log — removing the inner block would leave a dead outer scope that sets `m_debug_logged_remote = true`, computes `mask` / `out_idx` / `flags`, and discards them.
- **Fix:** Delete the entire outer `if` block. After also removing `m_debug_logged_remote` (which has no other callers), no behavior is lost.
- **Files modified:** `src/core/njclient.cpp` — extended deletion scope.
- **Commit:** `bec421e`.

### Deviation 3: `[Plan-faithful expansion] writeUserChanLog body fully deleted, not #if 0'd`

- **Found during:** Task 1 — applying Codex per-plan delta.
- **Issue:** The plan offered two options: `#if 0`-wrap the body (option a — for git-blame preservation) OR delete outright (option b). The plan said "prefer (a) — simplest" for the header, ambiguously left the body `#if 0`'d.
- **Fix:** Deleted the body outright (matching the header treatment). Replaced with a 6-line traceability comment that captures the same blame-history intent without retaining inert code. This is closer to Codex's intent (no inert-code retention) and produces fewer dead-code-detection false positives in static analysis.
- **Files modified:** `src/core/njclient.cpp`.
- **Commit:** `bec421e`.
